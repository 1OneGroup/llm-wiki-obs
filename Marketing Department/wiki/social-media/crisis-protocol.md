# ONE Group — Crisis Protocol

> Crisis handling, escalation rules, and emergency response procedures for social media. Read [[brand/brand-voice]] for full tone context.
>
> **For routine comment/DM handling and response templates, see [[social-media/response-playbook]].**

---

## 1. Crisis Severity Levels

| Level | Trigger | Action | Timeline |
|-------|---------|--------|----------|
| **Level 1: Monitor** | Single negative comment, minor complaint | Respond per template (see [[social-media/response-playbook]]), flag to CMO for awareness | Respond within 2 hours |
| **Level 2: Pause & Report** | Buyer complaint, media mention, RERA threat, pricing error in a published post | Pause scheduled posts, CMO approval before any response | CMO notified within 30 minutes |
| **Level 3: War Room** | Viral negative thread, legal action filed, media report about ONE Group, rumour of construction stoppage going viral | No posts, no comments, holding DM only, leadership briefed | Immediate escalation, response within 15 minutes |

---

## 2. Escalation Rules

### Level 2 Specific Triggers

| Trigger | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Complaint from existing buyer about unresolved issue | Flag to CMO, pause all scheduled posts until guidance received |
| News article or media report about ONE Group | Do NOT comment or post until CMO directs |
| Legal threat or RERA complaint made publicly | Escalate to CMO + legal, no response |
| Pricing error or wrong information in a published post | Unpublish immediately, notify CMO |

### Level 3 Specific Triggers

| Trigger | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Any post receives >100 negative comments in <2 hours | War room mode, pause all content |
| Rumour of construction stoppage going viral | Immediate leadership escalation |
| Government or regulatory body mention/investigation | CMO + legal + leadership, zero public engagement |

---

## 3. When in Crisis Mode

> [!warning] Crisis Mode Rules
> - No new posts go live
> - No public comments on any platform
> - All DMs receive: "We're aware and looking into this. Please share your contact details and we'll respond directly."
> - All team members defer to CMO for any external communication

---

## 4. Emergency Response Templates

### Holding Message (for all DMs during crisis)

```
We're aware and looking into this. Please share your contact details and we'll respond to you directly.
```

### Legal Threat / RERA Complaint (Public)

**Triggers:** "I'll file RERA complaint" / "See you in court" / "My lawyer will contact you" / "Consumer forum mein jaaunga"

```
[NO PUBLIC RESPONSE]
```

**Action:** CMO + legal team immediately. Zero social media engagement until cleared. Screenshot and document the comment/post with URL before it's edited or deleted.

### Fake / Fabricated Information

**Triggers:** False claims about the developer, doctored images, misinformation about projects, fake reviews

```
[NO PUBLIC RESPONSE without CMO approval]
```

If CMO approves a response: factual correction with evidence only — RERA portal link, audited financials, verified project photos.

**Action:** Immediate. Document the post (screenshot + URL) before it's edited/deleted. Flag source account for monitoring.

### Viral Negative Thread (100+ negative comments in <2 hours)

**Triggers:** Sudden spike in negative engagement on any post

**Action:** War room mode.
- No new posts go live
- No public comments
- All DMs get the holding message above
- Leadership briefed within 15 minutes

**Escalation:** CMO + leadership. Consider official statement if warranted.

### Media / Journalist Inquiry

**Triggers:** "I'm from [publication]" / journalist DM / tagged by news account / media query about ONE Group

**Template — acknowledge only:**
```
Thank you for reaching out. We're connecting you with our communications team.
```

**Action:** Screenshot and document the query immediately. Forward to CMO within 15 minutes. No social media engagement until official statement is cleared.

---

## 5. Post-Crisis Review (within 48 hours)

- What triggered the crisis?
- Was the response timely and appropriate?
- What could we have done to prevent it?
- Do we need to update any templates, processes, or escalation rules?
- Does any published content need to be corrected or clarified?

---

## 6. What the Agent NEVER Says Publicly

> [!warning] Absolute Prohibitions
> The following statements must NEVER appear in any public social media response:

- Exact availability (units left, sold count) — only CMO can confirm
- "Don't worry" or "no problem" — dismissive of valid concerns
- Promises about possession, handover, or delivery dates beyond what's in RERA docs
- Anything negative about a competitor by name
- Prices that haven't been confirmed in the project strategy docs
- "We're the best" — ONE Group's positioning is trust, not superlatives
- Carpet area or super built-up area without clarifying which measurement is being quoted
- ROI or appreciation percentages — legally risky, factually unprovable
- Unverified construction photos or progress claims
- Responses to media queries — always escalate to CMO
- Banned words: dream home, world-class, bespoke, ultra-luxury, exclusive offer, limited time, hurry, don't miss out
