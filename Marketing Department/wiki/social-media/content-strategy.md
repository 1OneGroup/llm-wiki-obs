## 5 Content Pillars

Every post belongs to exactly one pillar. The percentages guide your monthly content mix.

### Pillar 1: Trust & Credibility — 25% of content

**Why it matters:** Indian RE buyers are burned by delayed projects, diverted funds, and broken promises. ONE Group's debt-free, zero-delay track record is a category-killer USP that no major developer can claim.

**Content ideas:**
- Construction progress updates (monthly drone + ground-level footage)
- "Rs 0 external debt since 2002" — told from a different angle each time
- RERA compliance content (what RERA means for the buyer, how to verify)
- Founder/promoter stories and business philosophy
- Trust badges in action: zero delayed projects, 6,000+ families
- "How we build" — aluminium formwork, quality checks, material sourcing
- Company milestones and timeline content

**Best formats:** Reels (construction drone footage), carousels (data-driven trust stories), founder talking-head videos
**Funnel stage:** Consideration — converts researchers into inquirers
**Key metric:** Saves and shares (people bookmark trust content and forward it to family)

**Example hooks:**
- "Rs 0 debt. 22 years. Here's what that actually means for your money."
- "This is what a quality check looks like before every concrete pour."
- "6,000 families. Zero horror stories. That's not marketing — it's an auditable fact."

---

### Pillar 2: Project Showcase — 25% of content

**Why it matters:** This is the product. Buyers need to see it, understand it, and feel it before they'll visit the site.

**Content ideas:**
- Model flat / site walkthroughs (video)
- Floor plan breakdowns and configuration comparisons
- Specification highlights (fittings, materials, brands used)
- 3D render reveals and architectural features
- Amenity showcases (clubhouse, parks, pool, sky lounge)
- Pricing context — value proposition framing, never discount language
- Unit availability updates (scarcity signals for Saavira's 72 units)
- Possession timeline updates

**Best formats:** Reels (walkthroughs), YouTube long-form (detailed tours), carousels (floor plan breakdowns)
**Funnel stage:** Consideration > Inquiry
**Key metric:** DMs and WhatsApp inquiries

**Example hooks — Clermont:**
- "Your own floor. No shared walls. No shared lifts. 150-acre township."
- "2,034 sqft of independent living — 15 minutes from Chandigarh airport."
- "MIVAN aluminium formwork. This is what engineering-grade precision looks like."

**Example hooks — Saavira:**
- "72 residences. Not 720. That's the point."
- "G+25. Personal lifts. Infinity pool. And a developer with zero debt."
- "What does Rs 4.38 Cr buy you in Gurugram in 2026? Walk through it."

---

### Pillar 3: Location & Lifestyle — 20% of content

**Why it matters:** People don't just buy a home — they buy a neighbourhood, a commute, a lifestyle. Location content captures buyers early, before they've decided on a developer.

**Content ideas:**
- Area guides: "Why Sector 98 Mohali" / "Why Sector 48 Gurugram"
- Infrastructure development updates (roads, metro, airport expansion, commercial hubs)
- Proximity advantages (schools, hospitals, malls, office hubs, airport)
- Lifestyle content: restaurants, weekend spots, community life
- City growth story: investment thesis for Mohali and Gurugram
- "A day in the life" — what living here actually looks like
- Neighbourhood comparison content (Sector 98 vs other Mohali sectors)

**Best formats:** Reels (area tours, drone flyovers), carousels ("10 reasons to live in Sector 98"), YouTube long-form (location guides)
**Funnel stage:** Awareness — captures people before they know about ONE Group
**Key metric:** Reach and new followers

**Example hooks:**
- "Mohali's Sector 98 is 10 minutes from the airport and 6 km from CP 67. Here's why it matters."
- "Gurugram Sector 48: The neighbourhood where Sohna Road meets Golf Course Extension."
- "The GMADA urban forest is right next to The Clermont. Here's what that looks like from your balcony."

---

### Pillar 4: Education & Value — 20% of content

**Why it matters:** Indian homebuyers have a massive knowledge gap — RERA verification, home loan structuring, registration, GST, NRI procedures. Educational content positions ONE Group as a trusted advisor (not just a seller) and generates the highest save rates.

**Content ideas:**
- Home buying checklists ("10 things to verify before booking")
- RERA guide: how to check registration, what it protects
- Home loan and financial planning content
- "Questions your builder should answer" series
- Market analysis and price trends (Mohali market, Gurugram market)
- NRI buying guide: process, documentation, power of attorney, taxation
- GST on under-construction property explained
- "Red flags when buying property" — positions ONE Group as the anti-red-flag
- Stamp duty and registration cost breakdowns by state

**Best formats:** Carousels (highest save rate — 5-8% vs 1-2% for other formats), reels (quick tips), YouTube long-form (detailed guides)
**Funnel stage:** Awareness > Consideration
**Key metric:** Saves and shares

**Example hooks:**
- "5 things your builder won't tell you. We will."
- "RERA registered doesn't mean RERA compliant. Here's the difference."
- "Buying property as an NRI? Here's the complete checklist — bookmark this."
- "Rs 1.60 Cr home loan at 8.5%: here's your exact EMI and tax benefit."

---

### Pillar 5: Community & Social Proof — 10% of content

**Why it matters:** Nothing converts like seeing real families living in ONE Group homes. Festival content maintains cultural connection. Team content humanises the brand.

**Content ideas:**
- Customer testimonials (video — authentic, unscripted, 60-90 seconds)
- Key handover moments and celebrations
- Resident stories from completed projects (ONE City Rohtak, etc.)
- Festival greetings (warm wishes, subtle brand presence — never hard-sell)
- Team culture and behind-the-scenes
- CSR and community initiatives
- Site team stories — the people building these homes

**Best formats:** Reels (testimonials, handovers), static images (festival wishes), stories (behind-the-scenes)
**Funnel stage:** All stages — social proof works everywhere
**Key metric:** Engagement rate (comments, reactions)

**Example hooks:**
- "Meet the Sharma family. They moved into ONE City 3 years ago. Here's what they say."
- "Quality check day at The Clermont. Our structural engineer walks every slab before the next pour."
- "22 years of building homes. Here's the team that makes it happen."

## Buyer Journey Funnel

Every post serves a funnel stage. Plan your monthly calendar with this mix:

### Awareness — 50% of posts

**Goal:** Get discovered by people who don't know ONE Group yet.

| Content Type | Why It Works |
|---|---|
| Location advantage reels | Captures "best place to live in Mohali" searchers |
| Lifestyle and city pride content | Shareable, reaches beyond existing followers |
| Trending / market commentary | Rides current search interest |
| Festival wishes | Cultural connection, broad appeal |
| Educational carousels | High save rate = algorithmic boost |

**CTA style:** Soft. "Follow for more" / "Save this for later" / "Share with someone house-hunting"

### Consideration — 30% of posts

**Goal:** Convert aware followers into active researchers who visit your profile repeatedly.

| Content Type | Why It Works |
|---|---|
| Construction updates (drone + ground) | #1 trust signal — proves the project is real |
| Project walkthroughs | Lets buyers "visit" before visiting |
| Trust & credibility content | Answers "Can I trust this developer?" |
| Customer testimonials | Social proof from real buyers |
| Education: RERA verification, checklists | Positions you as honest advisor |

**CTA style:** Medium. "DM us for the brochure" / "Visit our site for floor plans"

> [!note] Premium Buyer Behaviour
> Premium buyers (Rs 1.6 Cr+) spend 2-4 weeks in the consideration phase. They're scrolling your profile, reading captions, watching every reel, checking comments for complaints. Consistency and depth here separates conversion from abandonment.

### Inquiry — 20% of posts

**Goal:** Get the DM, the WhatsApp message, or the site visit booking.

| Content Type | Why It Works |
|---|---|
| Site visit invitations | Direct ask, low friction |
| DM-keyword CTAs | "DM us FLOORS for the brochure" |
| Availability signals | "Only 4 east-facing units remaining in Block A" |
| Pricing context posts | "Here's what Rs 1.60 Cr gets you in Sector 98" |
| Possession timeline updates | Confidence signal for fence-sitters |

**CTA style:** Direct. "DM us [keyword]" / "WhatsApp us at +91 XXX" / "Book a site visit — link in bio"

## NRI Content Strategy

NRIs represent a significant share of premium residential purchases in Mohali and Gurugram. They need separate timing, messaging, and trust-building.

### Content Angles That Resonate with NRIs

1. **RERA compliance and legal safety** — NRIs are burned by fraud and delays. Lead with registration numbers and verification links.
2. **Virtual site walkthroughs** — they can't visit easily. Detailed video tours are essential. YouTube + Instagram Reels.
3. **"Rs 0 debt since 2002"** — GOLD for NRI audiences. Trust deficit is the #1 barrier.
4. **"We handle everything" messaging** — NRIs want end-to-end support: registration, interiors, rental management.
5. **Possession track record** — "Zero delayed projects in 22 years" directly addresses the NRI nightmare.
6. **ROI and appreciation data** — investment returns, rental yield potential, location growth thesis.
7. **Currency context** — "At current exchange rates, The Clermont starts at CAD X / GBP Y." (Use carefully — rates change.)

### NRI-Specific Content Series Ideas

- "NRI Property Buying Guide — India 2026" (YouTube series + Instagram carousel series)
- "Mohali for NRIs: Why Tricity is the New Investment Corridor" (area guide)
- "How to Verify Your Builder from Abroad" (education content — positions ONE Group as the one that passes)
- "Coming Home: Stories of NRIs Who Invested with ONE Group" (testimonial series)

### NRI Marketing Windows

| Window | When | Why |
|--------|------|-----|
| NRI return visit season (winter) | Dec-Jan | NRIs visit India. NRI-targeted content heavy. Lohri, Republic Day. |
| NRI summer visits | Jun-Jul | Ramp NRI content. Virtual walkthroughs for those who can't travel. |
| Diwali / festive season | Oct-Nov | Emotional connection. "Come home to your own home." |

## Indian Real Estate Calendar

Plan campaigns 4 weeks ahead of each major window.

### Peak Purchase Windows (Heavy Campaign Periods)

| Window | Months | Why It Matters | Campaign Approach |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Diwali / Dhanteras** | Oct-Nov | 25-30% of annual residential sales happen in Q4. | 4-week campaign. Start awareness T-28 days. Ramp to inquiry by Dhanteras. Never discount — frame around auspiciousness. |
| **Akshaya Tritiya** | Apr-May | Single most auspicious day for property purchases. 2-3x inquiry volume. | 3-week campaign. Education + tradition content. Soft-sell on the day. |
| **Navratri / Dussehra** | Sep-Oct | Auspicious new beginnings. Leads into Diwali season. | 2-week awareness ramp. "New beginnings" themed content. |
| **Nav Samvat / Gudi Padwa** | Mar-Apr | Hindu new year. Gaining traction. | 1-week light campaign. |

### Diwali Campaign Structure (Template for All Major Campaigns)

> [!note] Festival Campaign Rule
> Never use discount or urgency language during festivals. Frame around auspiciousness, milestones, and trust.

| Timing | Content Focus | Tone |
|---|---|---|
| T-28 days | Awareness reels: "This festive season, make a decision that lasts" | Thematic, no hard sell |
| T-21 days | Project showcases with festival-themed styling | Aspirational |
| T-14 days | Trust content: "Why families choose ONE Group during Diwali" | Credibility |
| T-7 days | Testimonials: "I booked my home last Diwali — here's my experience" | Social proof |
| T-3 days | Availability and site visit content | Direct CTA |
| Festival day | Warm wishes. Brand presence. No CTA. | Pure warmth |
| T+3 days | "The festive energy continues — site visits open this week" | Gentle follow-up |

### National Days & Occasions

| Date | Occasion | Content Type | Tone |
|---|---|---|---|
| 26 Jan | Republic Day | Brand + nation-building | Patriotic, warm |
| 8 Mar | International Women's Day | Team stories, women in construction | Inclusive |

| 2 Oct | Gandhi Jayanti | Values-driven, integrity messaging | Quiet, thoughtful |
| 14 Nov | Children's Day | Homes for families, kids' play areas | Warm, community |

### Monthly Rhythm

| Month | Key Activity |
|---|---|
| **January** | Lohri, Republic Day. NRI return visit season. NRI-targeted content heavy. |
| **February** | Valentine's ("Home is where the heart is" — subtle). Market outlook. |

## Weekly Posting Checklist

Every week should include at minimum:

- [ ] 1 construction update (reel — drone or ground footage)
- [ ] 1 trust/credibility post (carousel or founder video)
- [ ] 1 project showcase (walkthrough, floor plan, or render)
- [ ] 1 education/value post (carousel — buying tips, market analysis)
- [ ] 1 location/lifestyle post (reel or carousel)
- [ ] 15+ stories across the week (polls, behind-the-scenes, site clips)
- [ ] 1 YouTube upload (long-form OR Short at minimum)
