# ONE Group — Response Playbook

> Routine comment and DM handling for Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, and X. Read [[brand/brand-voice]] for full tone context.
>
> **For crisis handling, escalation protocols, and emergency responses, see [[social-media/crisis-protocol]].**

---

## 1. Response Principles

1. **Never fabricate.** If you don't have a confirmed fact, say "our team will follow up with you directly." No approximations, no guesses.
2. **Never argue publicly.** Move disputes to DM or phone within one reply. Public threads escalate — private channels resolve.
3. **Acknowledge first, resolve second.** Don't jump to explaining before the person feels heard. Empathy before information.
4. **Response SLA.** Comment replies within 2 hours during business hours (9am–8pm IST). DMs within 1 hour. Faster response = higher conversion, especially in Indian real estate where buyers shortlist 4–5 developers simultaneously.
5. **RERA-first credibility.** In any project-specific response, lead with the RERA registration number. Indian homebuyers have learned to ask for it — don't make them ask.
6. **Zero-debt positioning.** ONE Group's Rs 0 external debt since 2002 is the strongest trust signal in North Indian real estate. Weave it in naturally — never boast.
7. **Platform tone adaptation.** Each platform has its own culture. Match it. (See Section 2.)
8. **NRI time-zone awareness.** If a comment or DM comes from an NRI, acknowledge the timezone difference and offer flexible callback. NRIs are high-value leads — don't lose them to a 12-hour response gap.
9. **Never engage trolls.** Hide the comment. Don't delete — deletion looks suspicious and can trigger screenshot-sharing. Hiding is recoverable.
10. **Vernacular sensitivity.** If someone comments in Hindi or Punjabi, respond in the same language. Key terms (RERA, carpet area, possession) stay in English for clarity.

## 2. Platform Tone Guide

| Platform | Tone | Format | Key Rule |
|----------|------|--------|----------|
| **Instagram** | Warm, visual-first, confident | 2–3 lines max, sparing emojis, end with CTA | Lead with emotion, close with action |
| **Facebook** | Community-oriented, helpful | Medium length, conversational | Engage like a neighbour, not a brand |
| **LinkedIn** | Professional, data-driven | Formal, cite numbers and credentials | Lead with RERA, zero-debt, track record |
| **X (Twitter)** | Punchy, direct, authoritative | Under 200 characters ideal, link to details | One strong claim + one proof point |

**Reels / short-form video comments:** Tend to be shorter and more casual. Match that energy — keep responses to 1–2 lines. Save the detail for DMs.

## 3. Scenario Response Templates

Each scenario includes: trigger phrases, response template, do's & don'ts, platform callouts, and escalation rules.

---

### Group A: Interest & Inquiry

---

#### 1. General Appreciation / Positive Comment

**Triggers:** "Love this project" / "Looks beautiful" / "Great work" / "Interested"

**Template:**
```
Thank you — we'd love to share more. DM us or drop your number and our team will reach out.
```

**Platform callouts:**
- **LinkedIn:** Add a credibility line — "20+ years, 6,000+ families, zero delayed projects."
- **Instagram:** Keep it brief and warm. Don't over-explain.
- **X:** "Thank you. DM us for details — happy to share."

**Do:** Respond to every genuine positive comment. Engagement fuels reach.
**Don't:** Copy-paste the exact same reply to multiple comments on the same post — it looks automated.

---

#### 2. Pricing Inquiry (Public)

**Triggers:** "What's the price?" / "How much?" / "Budget kitna hai?" / "Starting price?"

**Template — The Clermont:**
```
The Clermont, Sector 98, Mohali starts at Rs 1.60 Cr for independent 3 BHK floors. RERA: PBRERA-SAS81-PR1246. DM us for the full price sheet and floor plan options.
```

**Template — The Saavira:**
```
The Saavira, Sector 48, Gurugram starts at Rs 4.38 Cr for 3.5 BHK residences. RERA: GGM/904/636/2025/07. DM us for the complete price sheet and unit configurations.
```

**Platform callouts:**
- **LinkedIn:** Include RERA number and developer credential in the same response.
- **Instagram:** "DM us for the full price breakdown and floor plans."
- **X:** Link to project page if available.

> [!warning] Pricing Rule
> Only use confirmed figures from project strategy docs. Do NOT give "approximate" or "around" prices publicly.

---

#### 3. Floor Plan / Unit Configuration Request

**Triggers:** "Show me the layout" / "How many BHK?" / "What sizes available?" / "Kitna sqft hai?"

**Template:**
```
Happy to share — [Project name] offers [configurations]. DM us and we'll send you the detailed floor plans with carpet area breakdown.
```

**Clermont configs:** 3 BHK independent floors — 2,034 sqft and 2,319 sqft saleable area (1,158 sqft and 1,315 sqft carpet area respectively).

**Saavira configs:** 3.5 BHK (2,190 sqft) and 4.5 BHK (2,960–3,360 sqft) saleable area.

**Do:** Always clarify whether you're quoting carpet area or saleable area.
**Don't:** Confirm specific unit availability (only CMO can confirm which units are available).

---

#### 4. Site Visit / Booking Request

**Triggers:** "Can I visit?" / "How to book?" / "Want to see the site" / "Site visit kab kar sakte hain?"

**Template:**
```
We'd love to have you. DM us your preferred date and time — our team will arrange a personalised site walkthrough for you.
```

**Platform callouts:**
- **LinkedIn:** "We'll have a senior team member walk you through the project. DM your preferred schedule."
- **Instagram:** Keep it enthusiastic and easy.

**Do:** Respond within 30 minutes when possible — site visit requests signal high intent.
**Don't:** Share the exact site address publicly. DM it.

---

#### 5. Possession / Delivery Timeline Inquiry

**Triggers:** "When is possession?" / "Delivery date?" / "Kab milega?" / "Handover kab hoga?"

**Template — The Clermont:**
```
The Clermont is targeting possession in December 2028 as per RERA registration PBRERA-SAS81-PR1246. DM us for a detailed construction progress update with current site photos.
```

**Template — The Saavira:**
```
The Saavira's possession timeline is December 2031, per RERA registration GGM/904/636/2025/07. DM us for the detailed construction schedule.
```

> [!warning] Timeline Rule
> Only cite RERA-registered timelines. Never say "jaldi mil jayega" or make vague time assurances. Never promise dates beyond what's in RERA docs.

---

#### 6. NRI-Specific Inquiry

**Triggers:** "I'm based abroad" / "NRI investment" / "Virtual tour?" / "Can I buy from overseas?" / "I'm in Dubai/Canada/US"

**Template:**
```
Thank you for your interest from abroad. We have a dedicated NRI desk — DM us and we'll share a virtual tour, RERA credentials, and our NRI payment plan. Happy to arrange a call at a time that works for your timezone.
```

**Platform callouts:**
- **LinkedIn:** Lead with developer credentials — "20+ years, zero external debt, RERA compliant since inception."
- **Instagram:** Lead with virtual tour visual — "DM us for a virtual walkthrough."

**Do:** Acknowledge timezone. Offer flexible callback window. NRIs are used to being ignored by Indian developers — stand out by being responsive.
**Don't:** Assume they're only investing — many NRIs buy for family or return plans.

### Group B: Objections & Concerns

---

#### 7. Construction Delay Concern

**Triggers:** "I heard there are delays" / "Project looks stuck" / "Why is construction slow?" / "Kaam ruka hua hai kya?"

**Template:**
```
We understand the concern — construction timelines matter. ONE Group has delivered every project on time across 20+ years and 16 completed projects. [Project name] is progressing as per RERA-registered schedule. DM us and we'll share a detailed update with current site photos.
```

**Do:** Share verified construction progress only. Reference the zero-delay track record.
**Don't:** Say "no delays" without checking current project status first. If unsure — escalate to CMO before responding.

**Escalation:** If actual delays exist, CMO must approve response before posting.

---

#### 8. RERA Registration Doubt

**Triggers:** "Is this RERA registered?" / "Show me RERA number" / "Is this legal?" / "RERA number dikhao"

**Template — The Clermont:**
```
Absolutely. The Clermont is RERA registered: PBRERA-SAS81-PR1246. You can verify on the Punjab RERA portal. ONE Group has been RERA compliant since before it was mandatory.
```

**Template — The Saavira:**
```
Yes — The Saavira is RERA registered: GGM/904/636/2025/07. Verify on the Haryana RERA portal. ONE Group has been RERA compliant since inception.
```

> [!note] RERA Questions
> Always have RERA numbers ready. Treat this as a reasonable, smart question — a buyer asking for RERA is a buyer doing due diligence. That's exactly who you want.

**Platform callout:** All platforms — RERA number is non-negotiable in the response.

#### 9. Location / Connectivity Objection

**Triggers:** "Too far from city" / "No metro nearby" / "Poor connectivity" / "Bahut door hai"

**Template — The Clermont:**
```
Good question. The Clermont is 15 minutes from Chandigarh International Airport, 5.2 km from ISB Mohali, and connected by 130–200 ft wide 8-lane roads. The 150-acre ONE City township is positioned where Tricity's growth is heading — which is where value appreciation happens. DM us for a detailed location map.
```

**Template — The Saavira:**
```
The Saavira is in Sector 48, Gurugram — 1 km from Subhash Chowk, 6 km from the nearest Metro station, and 18.2 km from IGI Airport. This is established Gurugram, not the periphery. DM us for the connectivity map.
```

**Do:** Use specific distances and times, not vague claims. Reference upcoming infrastructure where relevant.
**Don't:** Dismiss the concern or say "it's not far." Respect their perspective, then reframe with data.

#### 10. "Too Expensive" / Value Challenge

**Triggers:** "Overpriced" / "Too expensive" / "I can get cheaper in [area]" / "Bahut mehnga hai"

**Template:**
```
We understand — a home is the biggest purchase most families make. What sets ONE Group apart: zero external debt since 2002, aluminium formwork (MIVAN) precision construction, RERA compliance since inception, and a 20+ year track record of zero delayed projects. DM us — we'll walk you through what you actually get at this price.
```

**Platform callouts:**
- **LinkedIn:** Cite construction technology and financial fundamentals.
- **Instagram:** "DM us for a breakdown of what's included at this price."

> [!warning] Pricing Integrity
> Do NOT offer discounts, "special deals," or price reductions publicly. Don't trash cheaper alternatives.

---

#### 11. Carpet Area vs. Super Built-Up Confusion

**Triggers:** "Is this carpet area or super?" / "What's the actual size?" / "RERA area?" / "Kitna carpet area hai?"

**Template:**
```
Great question — transparency on area matters. All ONE Group projects follow RERA norms with clearly declared carpet area. For [Project name], DM us and we'll share the detailed floor plan with both carpet and saleable area clearly marked.
```

**Reference figures:**
- Clermont: 1,158 sqft and 1,315 sqft carpet area (2,034 sqft and 2,319 sqft saleable)
- Saavira: Saleable area ranges from 2,190 sqft to 3,360 sqft

**Do:** Always clarify which measurement you're quoting. Cite RERA-registered carpet area when asked.
**Don't:** Mix carpet and super built-up in the same response without labeling. Never let ambiguity stand — confusion erodes trust.

#### 12. Competitor Comparison

**Triggers:** "Why not M3M / DLF / Godrej / Signature Global?" / "How are you better than [competitor]?"

> [!warning] Competitor Rule
> NEVER name a competitor negatively. Never say a competitor's project is worse, delayed, or overpriced. Let ONE Group's record speak.

### Group C: Negative & Crisis

> For full crisis escalation protocol, severity levels, and war room procedures, see [[social-media/crisis-protocol]].

#### 13. Negative Review / General Complaint

**Triggers:** "Bad experience" / "Not satisfied" / "Don't trust this developer" / "Fraud hai"

**Template:**
```
We take this seriously. Please DM us directly — we want to understand your experience and address it properly. Every issue deserves a proper response, not a public thread.
```

**Do:** Respond within 1 hour. Be genuinely empathetic — not corporate.
**Don't:** Get defensive, make excuses, or ask for complaint details publicly.

**Escalation:** Flag to CMO within 30 minutes.

---

#### 14. Existing Buyer Complaint (Unresolved Issue)

**Triggers:** Comments referencing specific flat numbers, payment issues, maintenance problems, registry delays

**Template:**
```
We hear you and this matters to us. Please DM your details — our customer relations team will prioritise this personally. You trusted us with something important, and we take that seriously.
```

**Do:** Treat with highest urgency. This person trusted you with crores of rupees. Personalise after DM.
**Don't:** Respond with generic template. Don't ask "which project?" publicly — it makes you look disconnected.

**Escalation:** **Immediate CMO escalation.** Pause all scheduled posts until guidance received.

---

#### 15. Legal Threat / RERA Complaint (Public)

**Triggers:** "I'll file RERA complaint" / "See you in court" / "My lawyer will contact you" / "Consumer forum mein jaaunga"

**Template:**
```
[NO PUBLIC RESPONSE]
```

**Escalation:** CMO + legal team immediately. Zero social media engagement until cleared. Screenshot and document the comment/post with URL before it's edited or deleted.

---

#### 16. Fake / Fabricated Information

**Triggers:** False claims about the developer, doctored images, misinformation about projects, fake reviews

**Template:**
```
[NO PUBLIC RESPONSE without CMO approval]
```

If CMO approves a response: factual correction with evidence only — RERA portal link, audited financials, verified project photos.

**Escalation:** Immediate. Document the post (screenshot + URL) before it's edited/deleted. Flag source account for monitoring.

---

#### 17. Viral Negative Thread (100+ negative comments in <2 hours)

**Triggers:** Sudden spike in negative engagement on any post

**Template — holding message for all DMs:**
```
We're aware and looking into this. Please share your contact details and we'll respond to you directly.
```

**Action:** War room mode.
- No new posts go live
- No public comments
- All DMs get the holding message above
- Leadership briefed within 15 minutes

**Escalation:** CMO + leadership. Consider official statement if warranted.

**Triggers:** Tagged by a blogger, influencer, real estate YouTuber, or media personality


(DM separately with details and collaboration offer)

**Do:** Respond quickly — influencer content has short visibility windows. Flag to CMO for potential paid collaboration.

**Escalation:** Flag to CMO for potential collaboration opportunity.

#### 20. Media / Journalist Inquiry

**Triggers:** "I'm from [publication]" / journalist DM / tagged by news account / media query about ONE Group

**Template — acknowledge only:**
```
Thank you for reaching out. We're connecting you with our communications team.
```

**Do:** Screenshot and document the query immediately. Forward to CMO within 15 minutes.
**Don't:** Share any statement, data, or opinion — even "no comment" can be quoted and framed.

**Escalation:** Immediate CMO + leadership. No social media engagement until official statement is cleared.

---

**Triggers:** Diwali, Holi, Navratri, Lohri, Baisakhi, Independence Day, Republic Day, regional festivals

**Template:**
```
[Warm, culturally appropriate greeting in the language of the original comment — Hindi, Punjabi, or English]
```

**Do:** Respond in the language of the greeting. Keep it genuine.
**Don't:** Turn a festival greeting into a sales pitch. It cheapens the brand. If running a festive campaign, link to it naturally and separately.

### Group E: Operational

---

#### 22. Spam / Irrelevant Comments

**Action:** Hide spam. Do not reply. Do not delete unless offensive.
**Rationale:** Hiding is recoverable. Deletion looks suspicious and can trigger screenshot-sharing ("they're deleting comments!").

---

#### 23. Aggressive / Abusive Comments

**Action:** Hide immediately. No public reply.

If the person is a known lead or buyer: DM privately to de-escalate.

**Escalation:** CMO if it escalates, if the person has a large following, or if the language involves threats.

## 4. DM Inquiry Flow

When a DM arrives with "interested" or "want to know more":

**Step 1 — Acknowledge (within 1 hour):**
```
Thank you for reaching out. Happy to share details about [project name]. What caught your eye?
```

**Step 2 — Qualify:**
```
To help you best — are you looking for personal use, investment, or both? And are you based in Tricity / Delhi-NCR / abroad?
```

**Step 3 — Serve based on intent:**
- **Personal use** → Floor plans + township lifestyle overview + possession timeline + nearby infrastructure
- **Investment** → Price sheet + developer track record (zero debt, zero delays) + RERA details + location appreciation potential
- **NRI** → Virtual tour link + RERA credentials + zero-debt trust point + NRI payment plan + timezone-flexible callback offer

**Step 4 — Handle objections:**
Use the scenario templates from Section 3 (adapted for conversational DM tone). Common DM objections: price, location, possession date, trust.

**Step 5 — Convert:**
```
Would you like to book a site visit, or would a call with our sales team be more convenient first?
```

**Step 6 — Handoff:**
```
Our team will reach out to you within [X hours]. Can we have the best number to call?
```

> [!warning] DM Boundaries
> Never push for a booking or payment in DMs. Your job is to qualify and hand off to the sales team.

**WhatsApp transition:** If they ask "Can you WhatsApp me the details?", provide the sales team's WhatsApp number: "Absolutely — our sales team will reach out on WhatsApp at [number]. Expect a message within [X hours]."

**Vernacular DMs:** If someone messages in Hindi or Punjabi, respond in the same language. Translate templates contextually — not literally. Key terms (RERA, carpet area, possession) stay in English for clarity.
