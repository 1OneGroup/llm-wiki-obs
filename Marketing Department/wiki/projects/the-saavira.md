# Marketing Strategy: The Saavira | LAUNCH

## Campaign Concept

- **big_idea:** "72 families. One tower. Zero compromises. Singapore reimagined on Sohna Road for the few who know real luxury is measured by how few neighbours share your sky."
- **rationale:** In Gurugram's crowded luxury market (DLF, Godrej, M3M at Rs 3-6 Cr), scarcity is the weapon: 72 units in single G+25 tower. Every buyer gets 1/72nd of exclusive community — personal lifts, infinity pool. Singapore positioning = international identity competitors lack. A great alternative for Independent Floors in Gurgaon.
- **positioning:** For Gurugram top-tier execs/entrepreneurs refusing 500-unit high-rises — ultra-exclusive 72-residence tower with Singapore-calibre living on Sohna Road's best-connected corridor
- **competitive_angle:** Competes on exclusivity not unit count. 72 vs 500+ typical. Personal lifts vs shared lobbies. 2-level clubhouse for 72 vs overcrowded amenity floors. Every feature amplified by scarcity math.

---

## Tone

> [!warning] Content Guardrails for Saavira
> - **Do:** Let numbers create exclusivity (72) | Sensory language (views from 25th floor) | Reference Singapore naturally
> - **Don't:** Never say "ultra-luxury" — show it | No "affordable luxury"/value framing | Don't list amenities — narrate experiences

- **character:** Quietly commanding. Private members' club energy. Confident, understated, precise.
- **sample:** "Your lift opens into your living room. Your balcony stretches fourteen feet into the Gurugram skyline. Seventy-one other families understand exactly why."

## Personas

### P1: C-Suite Upgrader

- **profile:** Senior corporate leaders in Golf Course Road apartment, frustrated by overcrowded "luxury"
- **age:** 42-52 | **income:** 80 LPA-1.5 Cr | **occupation:** VP/CXO MNCs | **family:** married, 1-2 kids school/college
- **motivation:** Home reflecting corporate ascent, not another anonymous flat in 400-unit tower
- **values:** Exclusivity over size, design over decoration, address prestige, time efficiency
- **pain_points:** Generic luxury apartment, crowded amenities, no differentiation from cheaper neighbours
- **media:** LinkedIn, ET, Mint Lounge, premium Instagram, design magazines, golf/gymkhana clubs
- **key_message:** "You lead teams of hundreds. You shouldn't live among them."
- **triggers:** Promotion/bonus, spouse visiting Singapore/Dubai, current building Phase 2 (more crowding), peer purchase
- **timeline:** 2-4 months | **budget:** 4.50-6.50 Cr
- **influencers:** Spouse, architect/designer friend, financial advisor
- **contact:** Private preview → one-on-one site visit (no groups) → RM follow-up
- **objections:** 0.8 acres enough open space? | Dec 2031 is 5+ years | ONE Group vs DLF/Godrej?
- **requirements:** 4.5 BHK, higher floor, personal lift, smart home, walk-in closets, unobstructed view

### P2: Entrepreneur-Investor

- **profile:** Business owner viewing real estate as lifestyle + portfolio. Scarcity = appreciation.
- **age:** 38-50 | **income:** 1 Cr+ | **occupation:** Startup/family business/franchise | **family:** married, multi-property portfolio
- **motivation:** 72 units in G+25 on Sohna Road = faster appreciation than 500-unit projects. Lifestyle upgrade or premium rental.
- **values:** ROI, scarcity premium, brand for resale, construction quality
- **pain_points:** Saturated luxury market, interchangeable projects, previous generic tower investments underperformed
- **media:** Business WhatsApp, property investment YouTube, LinkedIn, ET Real Estate, broker networks
- **key_message:** "72 units. On Sohna Road. At launch price. The math is simple."
- **triggers:** Launch pricing, broker recommendation, per-sqft vs Golf Course Road, RERA confirmation
- **timeline:** 1-3 months | **budget:** 4.38-7.00 Cr (may buy 2 units)
- **influencers:** Property broker, CA, business peers with RE portfolios
- **contact:** Broker intro → investment presentation (appreciation data) → fast-track booking
- **objections:** Per-sqft vs Golf Course Road? | Two units possible? | Lock-in/resale clause? | ONE Group delivery track record?
- **requirements:** Flexible payment plan, clear resale policy, construction milestone visibility, RERA docs

### P3: Global Indian

- **profile:** Returned from Singapore/Dubai/London, seeking international living standards in Gurugram
- **age:** 35-48 | **income:** 60 LPA-1.2 Cr | **occupation:** MNC senior roles (repatriated) | **family:** married, 1-2 kids, frequent travel
- **motivation:** Singapore-style positioning matches lived experience. Clean lines, smart homes, functional luxury — not chandeliers and gold lobbies.
- **values:** Design minimalism, functional quality, international benchmarks, community curation
- **pain_points:** 10+ Gurugram "luxury" visits — all overdone, undersized, or overcrowded. Nothing like international apartments.
- **media:** AD India, Instagram design accounts, MNC relocation forums, Internations, Singapore property comparison
- **key_message:** "You moved back to India. Your living standards don't have to."
- **triggers:** Model apartment visit (Singapore DNA recognition), spouse balcony approval, school proximity (GD Goenka/Pathways)
- **timeline:** 2-5 months | **budget:** 4.38-6.00 Cr
- **influencers:** Spouse, international colleagues, relocation consultant
- **contact:** Digital-first (website → 3D walkthrough → curated site visit) → RM with hospitality background
- **objections:** Renders vs actual finish? | Sector 48 prestige vs Golf Course Road? | Community vibe with 72 families?
- **requirements:** 3.5 or 4.5 BHK, clean contemporary design, smart home, international finishes, international school proximity
